Acarbose (Precose)

Acarbose is a medication used for the treatment of Type 2 diabetes, a condition where the body struggles to effectively use insulin, leading to high blood sugar levels. It is usually prescribed alongside a proper diet and regular exercise and sometimes in combination with other diabetes medications.

Arcabose is an oral medication belonging to the alpha-glucosidase inhibitorclass of drugs. The primary role of acarbose is to control the spike in blood sugar levels that typically occurs after eating.

It works by targeting the digestive process, specifically slowing down the activity of the enzyme alpha-glucosidase in the intestines. This enzyme is responsible for breaking down carbohydrates into glucose, the sugar that enters our bloodstream.

Doing so helps slow down the digestion and absorption of carbohydrates which helps prevent spikes in blood sugar levels after meals.

In the United States, acarbose is available under the following brand names:

Precose

Common side effects associated with acarbose may include:

Stomach discomfort

Bloating or gas

Diarrhea

Mild skin rash or itching

More serious side effects which should be reported to your healthcare provider immediately may include:

Severe constipation
Stomach pain
Severe diarrhea
Rectal bleeding
Persistent nausea or vomiting
Unusual bleeding
Easy bruising
Liver problems

When used together with insulin or other medication used to treat diabetes acarbose may cause dangerously low blood sugar levels (hypoglycemia). Signs of low blood sugar levels may include dizziness, sweating, confusion, weakness, rapid heartbeat, and blurred vision. If you experience any of these symptoms, seek medical attention right away.

The above lists of possible side effects associated with Acarbose are not exhaustive and other side effects may occur. If you experience any severe or persistent side effects while taking Acarbose, it is important to contact your healthcare provider right away.

Frequently Asked Questions About Acarbose

Do You Need a Prescription for Acarbose?

Yes, acarbose is a prescription-only medication that is used to treat type 2 diabetes, it is not available over the counter and can only be obtained with a doctor’s prescription.

Can You Get an Acarbose Prescription Online?

Yes, you can get an acarbose prescription online with care at home. Our telehealth service allows you to start an on-demand visit with a licensed physician from the comfort of your own home. During your virtual appointment, the doctor will assess your medical history, current medications, and symptoms to determine if acarbose is the right treatment for you.

Can I Get a Prescription Refill for Acarbose From care at home?

Yes, you can get a prescription refill for acarbose from care at home.
